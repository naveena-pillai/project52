Interesting Facts About Sharks

  • Sharks don't have bones, only cartilaginous tissues.

  • Shark skin feels like sandpaper.

  • Scientists determine the age of sharks by counting the amount of vertebrae they have.

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bears

  • They got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub on a hunting trip.

  • The first stuffed bear was created by a German toymaker, Margarete Steiff.

  • The smallest teddy bear was created by Cheryl Moss and it is 0.29 inches.

Interesting Facts About Pandas

  • The scientific name of pandas is Ailuropoda melanoleuca.

  • Adult pandas are around 4 to 5 feet long.

  • They eat about 25 to 40 pounds of bamboo a day.

Interesting Facts About Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are a part of the iguana suborder.

  • They have the ability to change skin color which acts as a form of communication.

  • The largest chameleon can be up to 27 inches long.

Interesting Facts About Giraffes

  • Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth.

  • Giraffes only need to drink water once every few days.

  • They can run 35mph over short distances.

Interesting Facts About Foxes

  • A group of foxes is called a skulk or a leash..

  • Foxes have whiskers on their legs and face.

  • Foxes are independent creatures and tend to travel alone.
